Bowen Zuo is a second-year Ph.D. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of California, Riverside, advised by Yinglun Zhu. His research focuses on large language models, with a particular emphasis on test-time scaling. He is currently working on diffusion large language models.

Prior to his Ph.D., he received his M.S. in Computer Science from the Georgia Institute of Technology, working with Larry Heck on digital humans, and his B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Washington, where he worked at ACME Lab with Scott Hauck and Shih-Chieh Hsu on high-level synthesis and machine learning acceleration.
